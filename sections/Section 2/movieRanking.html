Great Movies According to Martin [Jan 2025]

My top 5 Science Fiction Movies. (dark)

A Clockwork Orange (1971)

A disturbing look at free will, violence, and societal control set in a darkly stylised, hyper-violent future, all wrapped in intense psychological horror.

Production Costs: $2.2 million => Box Office Sales: $26.6 million

Alien (1979)

The ultimate blend of sci-fi and horror, this movie captures the primal terror of a deadly alien force and the claustrophobic isolation of space exploration.

Production Costs: $11 million => Box Office Sales: $104.9 million

Blade Runner (1982)

This film delves into the bleak consequences of artificial intelligence and what it means to be human, exploring existential themes in a dystopian, rain-soaked future.

Production Costs: $28 million => Box Office Sales: $41.6 million

The Matrix (1999)

A mind-bending exploration of reality, control, and rebellion, where the dystopian world is hidden beneath a seemingly perfect digital facade.

Production Costs: $63 million => Box Office Sales: $466.6 million

Children of Men (2006)

A relentlessly grim, near-future vision where humanity faces extinction due to infertility, capturing a society on the brink of collapse, riddled with violence and despair.

Production Costs: $76 million => Box Office Sales: $70 million
